Welcome to lytics - a twitch clips case study

Twitch is a livestreaming platform for content creators to broadcast showcase their content while as being able to actively communicate with their fanbase. Twitch has a feature in which viewers can clip segments of the live broadcast that allow other people too see content even though they were not watching it as it occured live. As a result, clips are a greatway for a streamer to gain exposure outside of their fanbase, which in turn may increase their fanbase.

The best place to gain exposure outside of Twitch, is through the subreddit livestreamfail, which is dedicated to Twitch clips. The reason behind this is becaues the vast majority of Twitch's viewerbase only watch the popular streamers rather than an unrecognized streamer. When a streamer has a large fanbase that is actively watching their stream, they gain much more exposure than a streamer who only has less than 10 viewers. Livestreamfail as a result, is the best place to gain massive exposure for their content.

Features for better user experience are in the development pipeline and I am currently exploring various methods to gain the most accurate results to the case study.
